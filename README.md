Manual counting of paper bundles in offices, educational institutions, banks, and reprography centres is a 
routine yet labour-intensive task that consumes significant time and effort. The process is highly 
dependent on human concentration and is therefore prone to errors such as miscounting, double counting, 
or skipping sheets. These inaccuracies can lead to operational inefficiencies, financial discrepancies, and 
delays in document handling. Although automated paper counting machines are available in the market, 
most of them are expensive, bulky, and specifically designed for high-speed industrial environments, 
making them unsuitable and unaffordable for small and medium-scale organizations. To address these 
limitations, this project presents the design and fabrication of a cost-effective, compact, and portable 
automated paper counting machine tailored for light and medium-scale applications. The proposed 
system integrates a roller-based mechanical feeding mechanism to ensure smooth and consistent sheet 
movement, an infrared (IR) sensor for reliable sheet detection, and an ESP32 (NodeMCU) 
microcontroller for real-time processing and control. As each sheet passes through the roller assembly, 
the IR sensor detects its presence and sends corresponding signals to the microcontroller. The ESP32 
processes these signals, increments the count accurately, and updates the result on a display unit. In 
addition to basic counting functionality, the system incorporates a target count feature that allows users 
to predefine the required number of sheets using an input interface. Once the set count is reached, the 
microcontroller automatically stops the motor, preventing overcounting and ensuring precise batching. 
Visual and audible indicators further enhance user interaction and operational convenience. Experimental 
evaluation of the prototype demonstrates consistent sheet detection, stable mechanical operation, and 
accurate counting performance across different paper types. The developed system effectively reduces 
manual effort, minimizes human error, and significantly improves productivity in paper handling 
operations. By combining affordability, reliability, and ease of use, the proposed solution serves as a 
practical automation tool for institutions seeking efficient document management without high
